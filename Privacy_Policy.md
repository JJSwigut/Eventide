# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: July 9, 2026_

Eventide is a tide and marine-conditions Android app. This policy describes what the app stores on your device, what it sends to third-party services, and what the developer does not collect.

## Developer Collection

Eventide does not send personal data to developer-operated servers. The developer does not run an analytics, advertising, account, or telemetry backend for Eventide.

## Local App Storage

Eventide stores app data locally on your device so the app can work between launches. This can include:

- Cached NOAA tide station metadata and a last-updated timestamp.
- Favorite stations, home station, tide-alert preferences, and display settings.
- Android system scheduling data needed for tide alert notifications.

You can remove this local data by clearing Eventide's app data or uninstalling the app.

## Location and Station Coordinates

Eventide may request coarse location permission. When granted, the app uses the device location to center the map or help you find nearby tide stations. Eventide does not store your device location on developer servers.

When you view a station, Eventide uses station coordinates or the selected point to request tide predictions, weather forecasts, alerts, map overlays, and marine observations from third-party services.

## Third-Party Services

Eventide makes direct network requests from your device to third-party services. Those services may receive request details such as your IP address, user agent, request time, selected station IDs, map tile requests, and latitude/longitude coordinates needed to answer the request.

Eventide currently uses:

- NOAA CO-OPS for tide station metadata, tide predictions, and supported station observations.
- National Weather Service / weather.gov for forecasts, active alerts, and weather map layers.
- National Data Buoy Center for active buoy metadata and nearby buoy readings.
- NOAA nowCOAST for radar and satellite/cloud map overlays.
- Google Maps and Google Play services for map display and Android location services.

These services are governed by their own privacy practices and network logging policies.

## Alerts and Notifications

Tide alerts are scheduled by the Android system on your device. Eventide uses your selected station and alert preferences to calculate and show local notifications.
